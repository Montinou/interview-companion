# Job Profile: QA Automation Engineer

## Position Summary
Remote QA Automation Engineer for LATAM team with advanced JavaScript/TypeScript proficiency and Playwright expertise.

## Core Requirements

### Technical Skills (Must Have)
- ✅ **Playwright**: 3+ years hands-on experience
- ✅ **JavaScript/TypeScript**: Advanced proficiency
- ✅ **Asynchronous Programming**: Strong understanding
- ✅ **OOP Principles**: Solid grasp of TypeScript fundamentals
- ✅ **Page Object Model (POM)**: Experience designing and implementing POM-based frameworks
- ✅ **Data-Driven Testing**: Proficiency in data-driven testing techniques
- ✅ **CI/CD Integration**: GitHub Actions and/or Azure DevOps
- ✅ **English**: Advanced communication skills (written + verbal)
- ✅ **Location**: Based in Latin America, remote availability

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain automated test scripts (Playwright)
- Apply data-driven testing methodologies
- Utilize Page Object Model design approach
- Collaborate with development teams on test strategies
- Integrate test automation with CI/CD pipelines
- Write clear, concise test plans and test cases

### Nice to Have
- Experience with other test automation tools/frameworks
- Familiarity with agile development methodologies
- Background in QA for complex UI/UX flows

## Scoring Scale

### Per Criterion (1-10)
- **1-3**: Does not meet requirements / Significant gaps
- **4-5**: Partially meets requirements / Some gaps
- **6-7**: Meets requirements / Minor gaps
- **8-9**: Exceeds requirements / Strong candidate
- **10**: Exceptional / World-class

### Overall Score Interpretation
- **0-40**: No Hire (Strong No)
- **41-55**: No Hire (gaps too significant)
- **56-65**: Maybe (borderline, depends on team needs)
- **66-75**: Hire (solid candidate)
- **76-85**: Strong Hire (excellent candidate)
- **86-100**: Exceptional Hire (unicorn)

## Red Flags Checklist
- [ ] Weak English communication (below B2 level)
- [ ] No POM experience or understanding
- [ ] No CI/CD integration experience
- [ ] Data-driven testing not mentioned or understood
- [ ] Cannot explain WHY behind technical decisions
- [ ] Lack of collaboration experience with developers
- [ ] High job turnover without clear rationale
- [ ] No curiosity about new tools/approaches
- [ ] Rigid mindset ("this is how I've always done it")
- [ ] Cannot articulate test strategy beyond "write tests"

## Green Flags Checklist
- [ ] Proactive initiative (led migrations, built frameworks from scratch)
- [ ] Can explain WHY behind every technical decision
- [ ] Strategic QA thinking (risk-based, shift-left, ROI-driven)
- [ ] Strong collaboration with devs (not adversarial)
- [ ] Self-directed learning (side projects, new tools)
- [ ] Solid debugging methodology
- [ ] Thoughtful about AI/tooling integration
- [ ] Understands 100% automation is unrealistic
- [ ] Involved in QA from analysis phase (not just execution)
- [ ] Can mentor others or lead initiatives

## Interview Structure (60 min)

### 1. Warm-Up (5 min)
- Introduction, background, current role
- Ice breaker, rapport building

### 2. English Validation (10 min)
- **CRITICAL**: Switch to English early
- Non-technical topics: hobbies, weekend, travel, books
- Assess natural fluency, vocabulary, accent comprehension

### 3. Technical Deep Dive (25 min)
#### Playwright & Frameworks (10 min)
- POM implementation examples
- Test isolation strategies
- Cross-browser testing approach
- Fixtures vs seeders

#### CI/CD & Integration (8 min)
- Pipeline setup (GitHub Actions/Azure DevOps)
- Test execution strategy (smoke, regression, tags)
- Optimization techniques

#### Data-Driven & Strategy (7 min)
- Test data management approach
- What to automate vs manual
- Risk-based testing philosophy

### 4. Experience & Projects (10 min)
- Best project you've worked on
- Biggest technical challenge
- What did you improve in your last role?
- Example of a bug you're proud of catching

### 5. Strategic Thinking (7 min)
- How do you decide what to automate?
- How do you involve QA in the SDLC?
- How do you measure automation ROI?
- Shift-left testing experience

### 6. Career & Motivation (3 min)
- Why looking for a change?
- What excites you about this role?
- Long-term career goals
